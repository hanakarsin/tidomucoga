Karua loses again after High Court upholds Waiguru's win

 Posted on February 23, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  311 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- Justice Lucy Gitari ruled that the 2017 election of Waiguru was free and fair

- She dismissed Karua's petition and asked her to pay costs totaling to KSh 5 million

- This is the second time the court dismissed Karua’s petition against Waiguru’s win

High Court has for the second time dismissed Narc-Kenya Party leader Martha Karua's petition against August 8, 2017, election victory of Governor Ann Waiguru

Justice Lucy Gitari ruled on Thursday, June 14, the election was free and fair while citing lack of merit.

The judge added the claims by Karua that the election was marred with illegalities and irregularities were unsubstantiated and based on hearsay.

Karua has however promised to appeal the High Court's decision.

"We are determined to pursue justice to the last mile," she said.

This is the second time the High Court has dismissed Karua’s efforts to have Waiguru’s election victory nullified.

The case was first dismissed on November 15, 2017, by Justice Lucy Gitari who termed the petition "hopeless, defective and incurable".

Karua moved to the Court of Appeal which ruled in her favour, ordering that the case be heard afresh in the High Court.
