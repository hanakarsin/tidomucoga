Kenya's fast-rising and youthful political party, the National Reconstruction Alliance(NRA) has proven to Kenyans that it is the outfit that most people have all along been waiting for.

This is after the party, rather its officials landed an invite to Tanzania by the country's offiial opposition party Chama cha Demokrasia na Maendeleo(CHADEMA) invited several members of the unit to TZ for a benchmarking session.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt CHADEMA recently hosted top members of NRA in the country after they were pleased by the visionary and transformative agenda of the sensational and revolutionary National Reconstruction Alliance(NRA).

This media understands the invite came through after CHADEMA leadership got impressed by what NRA has been doing in Kenya.

According to Tanzanian media, the country's top opposition politician Tindu Lissu was pleased that young people would start a political movement to liberate their generation from poverty and unemployment.

Lissu's belief in NRA led him to challenge CHADEMA leadership to nurture young leaders who will value their youths with the aim of strengthening their youth league.

Sources close to CHADEMA intimated that the political exchange programme between National Reconstruction Alliance and the Tanzanian opposition party will ensure the two parties work on strengthening various party organs with a view of being a formidable party in the near future.

TUKO.co.ke understands CHADEMA officials, led by its Chairman Tindu Lissu were impressed by NRA's agenda that puts youths in the forefront, matter development and empowerment.

The same was echoed by one of NRA's party officials, Riziki Dunstan who noted that they were pleased with the invite by CHADEMA.

Riziki reiterated that NRA party officials will be visiting Tanzania before the end of July, just but to exchange ideas with the country's top opposition leaders.

The NRA official added that the party is looking forward to launching a nationwide campaign to popularise the party.

TUKO.co.ke earlier reported that NRA party officials on Tuesday, April 20, afternoon met Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi.

NRA officials met the presidential candidate at the Musalia Mudavadi Center in Nairobi.

According to sources intimate to NRA, the leaders held a closed-door meeting with Mudavadi to deliberate a number of pertinent issues.

Among issues that were discussed during the meeting include ways in which leaders can work together to improve the economy and political environment in the country ahead of the upcoming 2022 General Election.

The meeting between the NRA party leaders and Musalia Mudavadi comes barely two months after the party officially launched and unveiled it.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the fast-rising political party was officially unveiled on Thursday, February 11.

The unveiling ceremony was held at the Serena Hotel, Nairobi and saw the attendance of many people, including party leaders such as Amemba Magufuli, Riziki Dunstan, Fredrick Ojiambo and a host of other youthful political leaders who are on the verge of shaping the country’s political sphere.
