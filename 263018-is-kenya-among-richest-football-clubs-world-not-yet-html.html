Sports is one of the most thrilling and trending sector globally with a large fan base. Football is among the sports that have rampantly gained immense popularity across states. Internationalization of football games has attracted several interest groups for support and sponsorship. As a result of these dynamics, several clubs have developed and trained for high performance. Football Money League released by Deloitte clearly outlines the overall wealth status of various football clubs. Further, the report indicates the richest football clubs in the world.

According to the report, Manchester United emerged the first club to surpass a £500 million turnover. As a result the club went ahead of Real Madrid which holds a legacy of top place. For the last two decades, top three clubs have continually retained their places. Deloitte first launched the study of global clubs in 1997. In the past year, cumulative revenues for Money League clubs registered an increase of 12%. The latter rise in revenue for the top twenty clubs brought the totals to £5.5 billion.

Manchester United

Similarly, the report published by Deloitte pointed out that all Premier League Clubs will make their way up to the list of top 30 richest clubs in the world. In the past year, Manchester United emerged top in the list of richest clubs in the world with wealth estimated at £515.3 million.

Popularly known as the Red Devils, Manchester United has continually maintained its top place because of its surging status of wealth. The club emerged top for the first time in a decade under huge financial deals and sponsorship courtesy of Adidas and Chevrolet. The decision to return to the Champions League equally played a central role in their strong come back.

Barcelona

Barcelona came second in Deloitte listing with wealth worth £463.8 million. Bruce Springsteen played a central role to the good performance of this club. As the Boss, Bruce hosts 'The River Tour' a show that regularly earns the club about €8 million.
