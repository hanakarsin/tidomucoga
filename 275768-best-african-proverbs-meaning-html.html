Best African proverbs and meaning

 Posted on July 1, 2024  |  11 minutes  |  2300 words  |  Enda Jantzen

An integral part of the African culture is undoubtedly the African proverbs. There is something about the African proverbs that can really paint your life in a different picture. Some seem dumb on the surface, but when you meditate deep its magical how they touch on our lives. Another interesting part is that the proverbs have been in use for generations. The African proverbs with meaning are a colossal of emotions, ideas, reinforcements for arguments, message of inspiration, consolation and advice. They have been creatively crafted that without deep thoughts you can get lost on the surface meaning.Here are a number of proverbs for you.

One of the greatest authors to ever live in Africa, Chinua Achebe, once said that “proverbs are the palm oil with which words are eaten”. With every proverb comes a deeper meaning with a specific message intended for a particular audience. The proverbs touch on every aspect of our lives, from childhood, teenage hood, parenthood, and adulthood. It is a smooth cocktail with the main ingredients being wisdom with a shot of poetry. The beauty is when many people can relate to the meaning at some level. Some of the best African proverbs are a vessel of truth and a discovery of life lessons.

List of African proverbs

The worth of African proverb quotes are strongly crafted by the Yoruba’s of Nigeria who concluded that “A proverb is a horse that can carry one quickly to the discovery of ideas”. Bearing this in mind, we have compiled a comprehensive list of some of the most infamous African proverbs and meanings that touch on different aspects of our lives. The African proverb list touches on life and death, morality, trust, goals, responsibility, hard work and many more. Look at some of the awesome and educational African proverbs pics below:

African proverbs on hard work

“You learn how to cut down trees by cutting them down” – Beteke people

In life no one is born an expert. It is all through experience that one becomes a master of their own destiny. Do not be afraid of obstacles, challenges and failures as they are part of the journey to success. The world is a class and the lessons never miss. It is up to you to pick yourself and teach yourself how to make it happen. It speaks even in your job; you have to practice every day in order to earn an edge over others. We live in a competitive world and practice is the only way we can survive. So, start cutting down your trees, don’t let anyone cut them down for you.

“A horse has four legs, yet it falls often” – Nigeria

Many people in the world are afraid to pursue their dreams due to the fear of failure. A horse is used metaphorically as a strong person who is seen as successful in life but many people fail to see the many mistakes they have made in life. Even the strongest people in life sometimes have to take a blow and fall. It is all part of life, our fear to fail may be the reason we fail to succeed. So get on your feet, try out that new job you are afraid won’t succeed. We all start from humble beginnings.

“We share the same sun but not our homes” – Kalenjin proverb, Kenya

We all have the same opportunities to succeed in life. The sun shines for everyone, it is however up to us to how we choose to use the sun. Since when darkness starts to crawl it is all we have made in the sun that would be taken home. Work hard when the time is still right and later you can busk in the sun as you bask in the glory of your hard work as you reap the benefits. Wealth is accumulated and not handed over. So continue with that job, save up and focus on improving the quality of your lie.

African proverbs on responsibility

“One knee doesn’t bring up a child” – Swahili proverb

Parenthood is a demanding job and requires the effort and contribution of both partners. There is a current trend that is slowly creeping in Africa is many single mothers and broken families. A child requires the presence of both parents in order for a successful childhood. This is a message to all about to be fathers and mothers that it takes much more to be called a parent than just blood.

“A person who carries responsibility also receives blame” – Kenyan

Responsibility comes with making important decisions in life which may lead to dire consequences and undesired results. However, wisdom and maturity is when you realize that the decisions are the cause and accept the blame. Only through this can one learn from the mistakes and become a better person n life.

“You won’t last long, so leave a legacy”- Somali proverb

We are all living for a certain purpose. This proverb challenges us to do good while we are still breathing. African national has been tainted by corrupt people and this is the legacy many people live behind. Life is too short to be living doing evil. Put a smile on a neighbor or stranger ad they will have a lasting memory of your deeds. During your funeral your eulogy will be based on the lives you were able to touch. This should encourage us to avoid doing bad things to others.

African proverbs about goals

“Wisdom is like a baobab tree; no one individual can embrace it” – Akan proverb

While on the quest for knowledge especially with education becoming a critical tool for survival in today’s world, we should not forget to use wisdom which is inborn. Wisdom is one of the goals that people have replaced with knowledge and it has really cost humanity. A great author once said “Knowledge is prideful and brags about how much it knows while wisdom is humble and always wants to learn more”.

“One should stab a hyena and then aim for a lion” – Oromo proverb, Ethiopia

It is important to have goals in life since it gives a sense of direction. But while on the mission we should target feasible goal first before heading to the complicated ones. It is the experience that you gain accomplishing the small goals that will help you attain your major goal. So if you thinking of becoming owner at the place you work start of as the assistant slowly head up to management and with hard work and resilience you will have that top office. It takes character and self-awareness to distinguish between the hyena and the lion is in life.

“A short person hangs his bag where his hand can reach” – Igbo proverb, Nigeria

You are overwhelmed especially with the pressures of the world in having the biggest house, the latest car model, the most beautiful wife, that you forget that you are still working to get there. A short man is metaphorical of you and your resources at the moment. Don’t stretch your finances in order to compete with others who have ‘longer’ arms and are better advantaged in life. Start slowly and grow yourself. You will finally have those log hands and you can stretch you bag further.

African proverbs on morality

“When a monkey becomes old, it is fed by its young ones” – Bemba proverb, Zambia

As young people we should strive to take care of the old people in society who are unable to take care of themselves. While on our pursuit for a better life, we should not forget our parents back at home. They raised us to take care of them while in their old days. So drop all that work, take a ride back home and pay a courtesy visit to your folks. It is a sign of blessing and proves you don’t forget where you came from.

“It is not necessary to blow out the other person’s lantern to let yours shine” – Swahili proverb, Tanzania

We are all on the road to achieve success, on our way we met friends and make relationships. In case we met some luck we should use that to help others to reach where we are. Growth is when we help others achieve their dreams. Self-growth is an important skill that helps us boost ourselves. So don’t hesitate to help that assistant to become a director. We should always be ready to offer a lending hand to others since it will be next to helping ourselves out.

“If you can’t resolve your problems in peace, you can’t solve war” – Kenyan proverb

We are living in times of war between families, friends, and even countries. People have forgotten the sweetness of peace and always resolve to war as the first solution. We lack empathy and don’ care the people that will be hurt along the way. This proverb is meant to stop the resolution to war since there are no known ways to solve the war if it is not through peace.

“A calf doesn’t laugh at a hornless cow” – Ghanaian proverb

The future is a mystery and no one what lies ahead. So we should never laugh at some of those who are ahead of us when they fail. In case your fellow partner fails at succeeding at something we should take initiative and help them up as we learn more from them. That way we can gain the experience so that when it reaches our time we won’t also fail. We are the calves that have no experience and those ahead of us had the experience but unfortunately failed.

Wise African proverbs about women

“Instruct a man, you instruct an individual. Instruct a woman, you instruct a nation” – Moroccan proverb

Women especially in Africa have always been denied leadership roles by the male counterparts. Even up today there exist male chauvinists who still think that a woman place is in the kitchen. But many people recognize the power that a woman holds when they are handed over the power to rule there are many changes that can occur. Women are at the pinnacle of success of any nation. There needs to be a change in the leadership style in Africa to hand over power to women since many of the male leaders have failed in their work.

“A home without a woman is like a barn without a cattle” – Angolan proverb

Women are the pillar stones of any family. A family unit can’t stand without a woman who guides and takes care of the family members. Women make sacrifices in order to the sake of the family members. Their role has been respected since a long time ago even though there contribution has not been recognized to the fullest extent. It is high time we appreciate the effort of the women in our life and the struggles they go through in order to keep the family as a unit.

Thoughtful African proverbs about love

“The old woman looks after the child to grow its teeth and the young one in turn looks after the old woman when she loses her teeth” – Akan proverb, Ghana

The motherly instincts of a woman are praised here. It reflects on the caring attitude and persona of women that cuts through all generations as it lasts their entire lives. Women are painted as the thread that keeps the family together. It also speaks of the mutual relationship and responsibility that exists between a woman and her child. It is the rule for the woman to look after the child when young so that the young one can take care of the woman when she is old.

“When you educate a man you educate an individual, when you educate a woman you educate a generation” – Nigerian proverb

Education in today’s world has shifted and focused on the girl child. It has been proven that an educated girl will benefit his society since they are directly related to the family. They have feeling ad emotions and work extra hard to improve their society and well-being. This has been proved in today’s society where many educated women are making differences in the different sectors of the economy.

African proverbs about trust

“If you want to go quickly, go alone. If you want to go far, go together” – African proverb

It speaks on the importance of trusting others to come together to accomplish a common goal. You have to trust others in order for you to achieve a common goal together. Milestones are accomplished by a group of people with a common dream.

“A close friend can become a close enemy” – African proverb

It is important to check the people you chose to be in your inner circle. Jealousy can turn a close friend into your worst enemy. So keep mind of who you reveal your secrets in case they decide to turn against you.
