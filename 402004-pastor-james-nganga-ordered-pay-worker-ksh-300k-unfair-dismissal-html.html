Pastor James Ng'ang'a of the Neno Evangelism centre has been ordered by the court to pay a former worker KSh 300,000 for unfair dismissal.

The court heard that the man named Christopher Kinama who worked as a church caretaker, was sacked by the cleric in 2017 over an alleged car park fees collection dispute.

Kinama had after his firing, sued Neno Evangelism for compensation and got his share of the bargain when Justice Maureen Onyango ruled in his favour.

The judge found Pastor Ng'ang'a gave contradictory statements in his submission to the court on the exact date when his employee was removed from his job.

Whereas the church claimed it recalled Kinama after his firing, the letter was dated November 18, 2016, yet the employee was sent home on January 25, 2017.

“With utmost respect to apostle James Ng'ang'a, the author of the said letter, I am not convinced that it was humanly possible for him to predict that such events would take place and write the letter in advance. I have carefully perused the said letter. "It is dated November 18, 2016. As indicated in the pleadings, the events that led to the claimant’s termination occurred on January 25, 2017,” Onyango said.

Kinama started working at Neno in 2007 as a caretaker where he took home KSh 8,000 gross pay.

At the time of his dismissal, his salary had been increased to KSh 15,000.

Pastor Ng'ang'a's life has been shrouded in controversy and has often found himself in people's bad books for the past year.

The man of cloth has been described as rude, condescending and too harsh when it comes to his flock.
