DCI issues warrant of arrest against Wendi Waeni's ex-manager Joe Mwangi

 Posted on April 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  313 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) has ordered for the arrest of Joe Mwangi, the former manager to young talented acrobat, Wendi Waeni.

The DCI in a tweet on Friday, August 30, said it had obtained evidence that Joe had forged documents purporting to be from a cabinet secretary and was threatening the gymnast's mother.

According to the sleuths, the ex manager was issuing threatens to Wendi's mother, Magdalene Mbele, who is the complainant.

"Mr Joseph Mwangi Nduta alias Joe whose photos appear below is wanted by DCI detectives in connection with the forgery of a letter purporting to be from a Cabinet secretary threatening the complainant, Magdalene Mbele of some consequences. To report at DCI Nairobi Area," said the crime busters.

The acrobat accused Joe of mismanaging and squandering all the proceeds she got from her performances.

Wendi, in an explosive and moving interview on Citizen TV, said she was still living in a deplorable condition in Nairobi's Huruma estate with her mother was selling cigarettes to pay for rent and other expenses.

She narrated that despite performing in many high-end functions and receiving payments and donations from top political and corporate leaders within and without the country, her life remained a misery.

Joe, however, in a series of tweets refuted the claims and accused the young acrobat of being used to tarnish his name and reputation.

He accused Wendi's mother of being an irredeemable alcoholic who was misusing the minor's funds.
