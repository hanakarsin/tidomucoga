NTSA logbook transfer application and charges

 Posted on July 12, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1585 words  |  Hettie Henneman

Logbook transfer of ownership occurs many times in our country. This happens mostly when someone wants to sell his or her motor vehicle. However, before the vehicle becomes yours, you are required to do Logbook transfer which will verify the car ownership. Note that the requirements of the National Transport and Safety Authority- NTSA logbook transfer differ from each category ranging from an individual to a company to business.

The agency has made transfer logbook NTSA so seamless through the Tims which has phased out the manual registration on vehicles.

The National Transport and Safety Authority was created through Act Number 33:- an Act of Parliament on 26th October 2012. The objective of forming the Authority was to harmonize the operations of the key road transport departments and help to manage the road transport sub-sector effectively and reducing loss of lives through road accidents caused by both motorists and pedestrians.

NTSA logbook transfer procedure

In Kenya, NTSA logbook transfer charges also vary depending on engine capacity and can be paid through the mobile money system. Once the fee is paid, the system will produce a two-part verification code. The seller will receive the first section and the second part goes to the buyer through their mobile phones.

Individually party has to enter the code on his or her Tims profile for the transaction to be completed. The system will then show that the NTSA logbook transfer has been completed.

The NTSA Tims logbook transfer online system lets real-time transfer of vehicles without necessarily presenting any physical paperwork to the regulator. There are many things involved in this whole process which we are going to look at today.

    NTSA logbook transfer fee

    NTSA logbook transfer charges have not changed from the previous year, 2017.

    Below are the rates of the charges in Ksh depending on the engine size of the vehicle

    • Not exceeding 1000cc: Kshs 1,660
    • 1001 – 1200cc: Kshs 1,890
    • 1201 – 1500cc: Kshs 2,065
    • 1501 – 1700cc: Kshs 2,410
    • 1701 – 2000cc: Kshs 2,695
    • 2001 – 2500cc: Kshs 3,845
    • 2501 – 3000cc: Kshs 5,055
    • 3001 and above: Kshs 5,915
    • A trailer with less than four wheels: Kshs 1,030
    • A trailer with more than four wheels:Kshs 1,890
    • Tractor: Kshs 1,030

    NTSA how to transfer logbook to a new party

    Below is the process of how to effectively complete NTSA Kenya logbook transfer via Tims.

    • Create an account on TIMS for logbook transfer NTSA. Note that there are genuine agencies that can help with the transfer.
    • Click on vehicle registration
    • Then proceed to apply for transfer of ownership
    • Click on create new button, type the registration number of the vehicle you want to transfer and click on inquire button.
    • Upload a scanned copy of the logbook.
    • Input the Id number and Kra pinof the buyer and click on the add button.
    • Tick on the disclaimer button.
    • A code with be delivered to your phone or email
    • Click next, add the page shows the basic information of the vehicle to be transferred including the details of the new owner.
    • Then submit the application
    • Make the payments for NTSA transfer of logbook via MPESA, credit or prepaid card or pay cash any KCB
    • Once the seller submits the application.
    • The buyer will continue to login in his TIMS account and approve ownership.
    • A verification code will be sent to the buyer phone
    • Key in the details of the person who will pick the logbook.
    • Indicate the station which you prefer to collect the new logbook.
    • The National Transport and Safety Authority will then process the new logbook in two week time.
    • You will have to leave them with the old one logbook while collecting the new one.

    Advantages of online transfer

    Tims NTSA logbook transfer of motor vehicles by brokers, personal owners and financial institutions to the online portal is meant to make the procedure easier and reducing the amount of time vehicle owners spend on the task to a matter of minutes.

    The use of technology in the management of road transport will eliminate fraud and corruption, provide a single source of material on road transport data, increase compliance with traffic rules and enforcement and enhance access and security to information pertinent to the transport sector. It has also simplified NTSA online logbook transfer which was once a tedious and long process.

    The best thing about the NTSA taking over the functions of vehicle logbook transfer from KRA is that you can accomplish some of the activities through the E-Citizen Platform online.
