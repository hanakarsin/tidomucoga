Newspapers review for January 19: Uhuru dismisses hustler-dynasty politics

 Posted on April 13, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  521 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

The newspapers on Tuesday, January 19, reported on the full-blown attack that President Uhuru Kenyatta launched on his deputy, William Ruto during a pre-recorded interview aired on Monday, January 18.

The papers also widely covered the war of words between Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka and Deputy President William Ruto over graft accusations.

1. Daily Nation

The Daily Nation revisits President Uhuru Kenyatta's speech on leading Gikuyu radio stations in which he told off Deputy President William Ruto for his aggressive anti- Building Bridges Initiative in his vote-rich Mt Kenya region.

In the pre-recorded interview aired on Monday, January 18, Uhuru urged his own backyard to support the BBI warning the region stands to lose if it rejects the document.

The head of state also dismissed the hustler versus dynasty politics terming it as destructive and divisive to the nation.

He rubbished fears voiced by Murang'a Senator Irungu Kang'ata's bare-knuckle letter which warned of an impending flop of the handshake document, and launched a spirited defence of the BBI during the interview.

2. The Standard

According to this publication, the Nyamira county assembly is set to debate Governor Amos's Nyaribo nominee for deputy governor James Gesami.

The appointment has already set the stage for a political duel between clans with Gesami's detractors viewing him as a possible threat to a section of the politicians.

Some key players from the Oranga Democratic Movement (ODM) are already lobbying for the appointee with the help of locals lawmakers.

Those against Gesami, who was appointed in early January, are lobbying for his rejection by the Members of County Assembly.

3. Taifa Leo

Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) secretary-general Francis Atwoli has lashed out at the Council of Governors (CoG) for the ongoing stalemate between the county bosses and striking health workers, Taifa Leo reports.

In particular, the COTU boss took issue with Governor Oparanya for his earlier threats in which he told striking health workers they would not get their salaries.

Atwoli warned Oparanya's remarks will ultimately deteriorate the already worsening health sector in the country.

He advised all county chiefs to bring to an end to the strike and restore dignity in the health sector.

4. People Daily

President Uhuru Kenyatta has warned school heads in both day primary and secondary schools against sending away students home for school fees, People Daily reports.

In an interview on Monday, January 18, Uhuru said action will be taken against the school heads that will defy the directive.

He said the government was fully committed to supporting all learners in day schools.

The president, did not, however, pronounce himself of boarding schools.
