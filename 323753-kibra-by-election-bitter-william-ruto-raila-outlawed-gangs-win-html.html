Kibra by-election: Bitter William Ruto says Raila used outlawed gangs to win

 Posted on March 19, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  451 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- Ruto said Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) had no strategy to win election other than using militia groups

- He regretted the ODM leader Raila Odinga had not learned anything from the handshake pact with President Uhuru Kenya

- A number of Jubilee leaders including ex-Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale and Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa were roughed up during the election

- Imran Okoth of ODM was declared winner with 24, 636 votes against 11,230 votes garnered by McDonald Mariga of Jubilee

A seemingly vexed Deputy President William Ruto has descended on ODM leader Raila Odinga accusing him of using violence, chaos and intimidation to win the Kibra by-election.

Ruto whose Jubilee candidate McDonald Mariga lost to ODM's Imran Okoth said the Raila-led outfit had no other strategy apart from using organised groups to mete terror on opponents.

The bruised DP who was responding to a headline on a local newspaper suggesting Raila used spies to monitor Jubilee campaigns, said the Orange party only unleashed militia and outlawed groups to intimidate voters.

"The only strategy and structure Tinga's party had which largely succeeded was, violence, chaos, mayhem and terror meted out by organized baba's militia the same instruments used by terrorist organizations including outlawed groups. How sad they have learnt nothing from handshake," he said.

Kibra by-election recorded a number of chaotic scenes with Jubilee supporters and leaders being major victims of the fracas.

Former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale, Langata MP Nickson Korir, Oscar Sudi (Kapsaret), Charles Jangua (Starehe) and Didmus Barasa (Kimilili) were among jubilee leaders who were roughed up by rowdy youths.

Khalwale who turned out to be the emblem of stone-throwing tussle was cornered by a group of charged youths while on his way to supervise the election at a polling centre within the consistency.

His bodyguard's efforts to whisk him away were futile as the youths pounced on him and started throwing stones at him.

When he realised the youths were not letting him out of the battlefield, Khalwale gathered stones and returned.

Barasa was also belted with goons at DC grounds and harassed after Dagoretti North MP Simba Arati threatened to evict him for allegedly bribing voters.
