Kisumu: Appellate court upholds death sentence against 7 men accused of killing 2 fishermen

 Posted on May 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  535 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- The accused were identified as Peter Otieno, David Ongata, Tilleny Odhiambo, William Ayugi, Silas Onyango, Tiberious Ododa and one unnamed

- They were accused of attacking and brutally killing Felix Ouma Odhiambo and Lameck Olima Ochieng in August 2010

- High Court found them guilty of murder charges after 15 witnesses, including eye witnesses, testified against them

- Aggrieved by the ruling, they moved to the Court of Appeal seeking to overturn the verdict

A Court of Appeal in Kisumu has upheld a death sentence against seven men convicted of brutal murder of two fishermen at Dhogunda Beach in Suba, Homa Bay county.

The court on Wednesday, July 10, found Peter Otieno, David Ongata, Tilleny Odhiambo, William Ayugi, Silas Onyango, Tiberious Ododa and another unnamed suspect not before court guilty of killing Felix Ouma Odhiambo and Lameck Olima Ochieng.

The three-judge bench comprised of Justices Asike Makhandia, Otieno Odek and Patrick Kiage ruled the high court did nothing wrong in convicting and sentencing to death the seven suspects.

According to the judges, some 15 prosecution witnesses, including eye witnesses, testified against the accused at the lower courts during trial saying they were responsible for the cruel murder of Ouma and Olima.

The seven accused allegedly attacked and murdered the fishermen on the night of August 17, 2010, using spears and stones after the deceased refused to hand over their night catch and cash.

Although they denied the murder charges and insisted on their innocence, the high court found them guilty as charged.

Feeling aggrieved by the high court ruling, the accused proceeded to the appellate court with the hope the verdict could be overturned.

At the court of appeal, the defendants argued the trial court did not consider all the evidence they presented in court.

They further argued the prosecution witnesses who testified against them gave conflicting accounts of what happened.

However, the appellate judges felt the suspects did not prove beyond reasonable doubt that they were wrongfully convicted of murder and consequently upheld the previous ruling.

In Kenya, death sentence is a capital punishment explicitly outlined in law and is meted out on convicts of murder, attempted and robbery with violence, treason, and oath-taking for crimes.

The last people ever executed in the country after such a sentence were Kenya Air Force officers Hezekiah Ochuka, Pancras Oteyo Okumu and two others who were convicted for treason after the failed 1982 coup.

Kenya has one special vacancy of a hangman which has not been filled for nine years now despite the huge unemployment rate in the country after chief executor, Kirugumi Wa Wanjiku, died of pneumonia in 2009.
