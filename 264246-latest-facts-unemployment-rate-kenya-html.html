Latest facts on unemployment rate in kenya

 Posted on March 12, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  753 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

According to employment experts, political insecurity during the August polls and October repeat polls were significant contributing factors to the increased rate of unemployment in the country. Emmanuel Mutuma, the Chief Executive Officer of Brighter Monday, expressed in a press release that there was a radical decline on job opportunities during the August polls. This was followed by an immediate recovery in September when job opportunities increased by 10% then later dropped again to a whopping 29% during the October repeat polls.

Definition of Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate in Kenya refers to the total number of individuals actively searching for jobs as a percentage of those persons currently in the labor force. In 2017, a survey conducted by the United Nations revealed that unemployment rate in Kenya stands at 39.1%. When compared to other East African nations like Ethiopia, Tanzania, Rwanda, and Uganda, Kenya seems to be on the forefront with a high unemployment rate.

Calculation of Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate labor as a percentage by taking the total number of unemployed persons and dividing it by the number of those in the civilian labor force.

Unemployment rate= total number of unemployed people/total people currently in labor force

Unemployment Rate in Kenya

Unemployment has proved to be an overwhelming challenge in Kenya’s social, economic development since the nation gained its independence.in fact, youth unemployment has been the biggest concern since only 11.4 million youth are in employment. The ministry of labor in Kenya points out that approximately 2.1 million individuals work for the formal industry while the rest 9.3 million are self-employed.

Types of Unemployment in Kenya

Seasonal unemployment

This type of unemployment occurs when a person is working routinely for part of the year and remains unemployed for the rest of the months or weeks. For instance, the tourism industry is prone to seasonal employment.

Frictional Unemployment

This is the type of unemployment that occurs when young people are moving from one job to another.

Structural Unemployment

This particular unemployment is caused by a technical change such as when new production automation is adopted or when there is a decline in the supply of goods and services.

Causes of Unemployment in Kenya

Several reasons lead to the high unemployment rate Kenya.

High population

It is believed that Kenya’s population rate is growing tremendously as compared to its economy. Presently, the United Nations estimates that there are 48,609,428 people in Kenya which is an increase of 10million from the last census conducted in 2009. Given this significant population rate, it is expected that there are more people than the current economy can sustain I terms of job opportunities; hence, leading to a higher unemployment rate.

Global economic recessions

Global economic recessions is another reason why there is a high number of unemployed people in Kenya as it had decreased the capacity of most economic sectors to create more job opportunities.

Irrelevant Education

The Kenya education system is notorious for offering unapproved courses or merely coaching scholars on how to pass exams without providing them practical skills that they will make them more productive after school. An example of an irrelevant course offered includes the Law school at Moi University which was closed down due to lack of accreditation by the ministry of education.

Capacity Under-utilization

Due to lack of demand for goods and services, most firms end up hiring fewer people than they are capable.

Imperfections in the labor sector

Government and labor unions involvements result in various imperfections in the labor industry which contributes to unemployment.

Seasonal Change

In Kenya, most job opportunities change seasonally. For this reason, there are moments when demand for labor increases for a particular season then declines when offseason and this result in seasonal unemployment. For example, most labour in rural regions is mainly periodic.
