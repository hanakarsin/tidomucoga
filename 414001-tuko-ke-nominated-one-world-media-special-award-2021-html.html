TUKO.co.ke Nominated for One World Media Special Award 2021

 Posted on April 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  499 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- TUKO.co.ke was the only Kenyan media platform that was nominated for the One World Media (OWM) Awards

- OWM recognises and celebrates the best media coverage of developing countries, focusing on stories that connect people across cultures

- TUKO.co.ke also recently became a member of the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) Media Compact

It is another moment of celebration for TUKO.co.ke after it was nominated for the One World Media (OWM) Awards 2021.

Out of the hundreds of applicants from across the world, TUKO.co.ke was the only Kenyan media in the list of top 10 nominees.

The first batch of nominees across different categories was announced in April. The final list was released in May. TUKO.co.ke was nominated in the Special Award category.

Managing Editor Julia Majale expressed enthusiasm following the nomination saying the recognition is proof that TUKO.co.ke is impacting lives and the world at large.

“This recognition by an international organisation increases our resolve to continue informing the public and providing a platform for Kenyan people to share their voices and stories, we are doing something right and impacting the world,” said Majale.

According to the 2021 OWM Awards jury, which brought together the industry’s most distinguished journalists, media professionals and filmmakers, they received hundreds of entries from 96 countries across the world.

Winners will be unveiled on June 17.

About OWM

One World Media is a UK-based non-profit organisation that recognises and celebrates the best media coverage of the developing countries, focusing on stories that break through stereotypes, change the narrative and connect people across cultures.

Founded about 30 years ago, OWM is the only organisation that recognises excellence in international journalism from the global south.

Every year, it invites journalists and independent media organisations specifically from developing countries to take part in the annual awards.

This was the second time TUKO.co.ke participated in the OWM Awards, as one of the leading media outlets whose stories have positively impacted lives of millions locally.

UN-SDG Media Compact

A month earlier, TUKO.co.ke had become a member of the United Nation (UN) Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) Media Compact. It was one of the only three Kenyan media companies that joined the programme.

It is a special opportunity that the UN extends to leading media organisations around the world to partake in the collective responsibility of making the world a better place as envisioned in the SDG agenda.

Our UN-SDG Media Compact membership and participation in the OWM Awards are clear indications that TUKO.co.ke is committed to excellence in journalism and coverage of stories that need to be told.
