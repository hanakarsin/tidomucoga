Tanzanian Head of State Samia Suluhu Hints She'll Run for Presidency in 2025

 Posted on August 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  448 words  |  Lourie Helzer
  • President Samia Suluhu of Tanzania said that her elevation to the top job was by the grace of God and constitutional requirements following Magufuli's death
  • Samia stated that a woman has never been elected in the East African country as a president
  • She said if Tanzanian women will work together, then it will pave the way for one of them to become the next president

Tanzania's president Samia Suluhu has finally confirmed that she will run for the country's topmost seat come during the 2025 General Election.

First elected female president

Samia said she wanted to become Tanzania's first elected female president since the country has never elected a female president.

“Women have not yet elected a woman president, what we have tried at least is having a female vice president, I am leading because of God’s wish and constitutional requirements," she said.

Speaking at an event to mark the International Day of Democracy in Dar es Salaam President Samia said her elevation to the top job was by the grace of God and constitutional requirements.

She said if Tanzanian women work together, it will pave the way for one of them to become a president.

“We have favoured men to become presidents for far too long. God has given us this special opportunity... if we don’t grab it with both hands, it will become like a curse upon us (women).They have started writing in newspapers that Samia will not run for the presidency; who told you I will not stand? If God’s grace comes into your hands, do not leave it,” she said.

She also reiterated her pledge to appoint more women to leadership positions to bridge the gender gap.

Samia's remarks came days after her significant Cabinet reshuffle, which included two more women among four new ministerial appointments.

TUKO.co.ke had reported Samia appointed a woman into the defence docket, making her the first woman to hold the position since independence.

Prior to her appointment, Stergomena Lawrence Tax served as the first and only executive secretary of the Southern African Development Community (SADC), an inter-governmental organisation established in 1992 to promote political, socio-economic and security cooperation and integration among its 16 members.

Samia said she appointed Tax to break the taboo that reserves the position for men saying her work will be to coordinate and manage the ministry.
