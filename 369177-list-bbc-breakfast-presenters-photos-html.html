List of BBC Breakfast presenters with photos

 Posted on April 8, 2024  |  9 minutes  |  1730 words  |  Lourie Helzer

News programmes have suddenly become hot property and are vying for attention with other popular programmes aired on different channels. All major television broadcasters are including at least one news channel to their bouquet. The BBC Breakfast show is one of the most-watched breakfast news programmes. Thanks to the BBC Breakfast presenters, it is the most viewed breakfast show in the world. Viewers from all corners of the world tune in to the show to have a glimpse of what is happening around the world.

BBC breakfast show dates back in 2000 when BBC one and BBC news 24 merged into a single simulcast called BBC Breakfast. Sophie Raworth and Jeremy Bowen hosted the first show. The breakfast show, as it is popularly known, is aired from Salford Quays, in the Nothern part of England. Viewers get updates on the latest news, sport, business and weather.

Are you interested in finding out more about the people who regularly present and report for BBC Breakfast? Here is a list of your favourite BBC breakfast news presenters who keep you glued to your TV every morning:

1. Louise Minchin

Louise Mary Minchin is a female presenter on BBC Breakfast. She presents BBC Breakfast on BBC One, which is the most-watched breakfast programme in the UK. The 51-year-old presents the show from Monday to Wednesday alongside co-presenter Dan Walker.

Louise started her career at the Latin American Section of the BBC World Service. She is also a guest presenter for The One Show. She is also a regular presenter for BBC News at One.

Besides anchoring, Louis is a well-known actress who featured in Torchwood and Silent Witness. She is married to David Minchin and is a mother of two. She has also written a book about herself titled Dare to Tri, which is an amazing story of her life journey from the breakfast show to representing Great Britain at the World Triathlon Championships.

She holds a degree in Spanish at St. Andrew's University and has trained as a radio journalist at The London College of Printing.

2. Naga Munchetty

Subha Nagalakshmi Munchetty Chendriah is a British Indian TV presenter, newsreader and journalist. As well as making sure that the audience stays informed during breaking and developing news stories, she enjoys asking questions that the audience would like to ask.

Her first job in TV was at Reuters Financial Television, followed by stints at CNBC Europe, Channel 4 News and Bloomberg Television.

Naga's authoritative coverage of major news events has won her many fans. Munchetty is married to James Haggar. Although the two do not have any children yet, they do share two Siamese cats.

Besides TV, the female presenter on BBC breakfast plays jazz trumpet and classical piano. She also plays golf and enjoys making cocktails, a skill she says she learnt while working in bars during her days at the university.

3. Dan Walker

Daniel Meirion Walker is one of the most famous breakfast news reporters. He took over from Bill Turnbull as the presenter of BBC Breakfast in February 2016. Dan has presented Football Focus since 2009 and BBC breakfast since February 2016. Before appearing on the Breakfast show, Dan presented the afternoon show on Radio 5 Live from Mondays to Thursdays.

Dan has covered a number of major Tournaments for BBC sports including World Cups and European Championships. He is married to Sarah Walker, and together they have three children.

His journalism career began at the Sheffield's Hallan FM Radio before he moved to Manchester's KEY 103 radio station. From there he went to Granada TV and BBC North West Tonight before eventually moving to BBC Sport and now BBC Breakfast too.

4. Mike Bushell

Mike Bushell is also on the list of BBC breakfast cast. Mike was one of the first faces on air when News 24 started in 1997 (now the BBC News channel), and still occasionally presents the sport on BBC News.

He has also presented programs on History and Entertainment. Last year, he was among the contestants in The Strictly Come Dancing show where he was paired with Katya Jones. He is married with three daughters.

5. Sally Nugent

Sally Nugent is one of the most famous sports presenters on BBC. She has been working for the BBC for 15 years. Sally joined BBC as a broadcast journalist at her local radio station and has covered many big stories, including the Grand National that never was in 1997. She moved to the regional newsroom, and when their sports presenter took a break, she stepped in and remained there for two years.

Sally covered the 2002 Commonwealth Games in Manchester. She then moved to national news covering many big sporting events including two FIFA World cups. Sally has currently taken a break to contest in The Strictly Come Dancing show. She is married with one child.

6. Carol Kirkwood

The award-winning weather presenter began her BBC career when she became part of the Secretarial reserve in London. Her training at the Meteorological department led to her joining the BBC Weather team in 1998. Currently, she presents the weather from a variety of locations on weekday mornings.

Carol is married to Jimmy Kirkwood, and they do not have any children yet. Besides presenting the weather at BBC, Carol has been seen trying out everything, from racing high powered speed boats to charity tandem ride for children in need. Recently, Carol was involved in a bike accident which left her knee injured, prompting her to stay at home and rest. She has recovered and is back on the screens.

7. Jon Kay

Jonathan Gordon joined the university to pursue a career in politics, but he later changed his mind and pursued journalism. He joined BBC in 1993 as a trainee reporter working at a local radio station from Newcastle to Suffolk.

After working in local radio and regional TV, in Bristol, he went to London to work as a political reporter and news correspondent. Jon joined the BBC breakfast team in 2010. He covers for Dan and Charlie when absent and works as a roving reporter for BBC News.

Jon has presented all kinds of programmes for the BBC. He has anchored Drive on 5 live, documentaries for Radio 4, the BBC Two environmental series It's Not Easy Being Green and a documentary about bread-making for BBC Four. Jon also covers stories outside his patch. He was the Olympics correspondent for BBC News at Six and Ten during the London 2012 Games.

8. Steph McGovern

Steph tops the list of BBC Breakfast business presenters. The award-winning broadcaster has been working in financial journalism for over 13 years and has been part of the BBC Breakfast family for the last five years. Steph travels all over the UK to cover economic and business news for the BBC. She has broadcast live from over 500 businesses.

Whether she's talking to workers in factories or interviewing FTSE 100 chief executives in the studio, Steph makes it her mission to find out what's going on in the real economy. This year, she launched two new shows; 'Shop Well For Less' and a children's show titled 'Pocket Money Pitch.' Besides broadcasting Business on BBC, she attends international dancing competitions, where she helps coach dancers.

9. Ben Thompson

Ben is also on the list of breakfast TV reporters. He began his BBC career in 2001 at Radio Wales. While studying at the university, he was introduced the network's morning news programme, Good Morning Wales, and was selected as a BBC Trainee Scheme. He has worked for BBC as a business news correspondent.

It's a job that's seen him interview world leaders, company bosses and a few showbiz names along the way. He currently presents on the Breakfast show where he co-hosts Business Live. Outside work, Ben enjoys travelling, photography, music and theatre.

10. Charlie Stayt

Charlie Stayt is on the list of BBC breakfast presenters Saturday. He began his career in Commercial radio in his hometown before moving to ITN where he began his TV career. Before joining BBC Breakfast, Charlie worked as a principal anchor with Five news. He joined BBC breakfast in 2006 as a relief presenter.

In 2007, he was confirmed as BBC's Friday to Sunday presenter. Since January 2008, he has been the weekend presenter for BBC breakfast. Charlie has presented several current affairs programs and has also hosted live reality TV shows Jailbreak and Are You Pathetic. He is married to Anne Breckell, and they are blessed with two children.
