Uganda Airlines returns to the skies with first flight to Nairobi

 Posted on March 27, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- The airline collapsed in 2001 and has been out of the market since then

- President Yoweri Museveni described its comeback as historic and joyous moment for people of Uganda and East Africa at large

- It is expected to make other flights in Mombasa, Mogadishu (Somalia), Dar Salaam (Tanzania), Juba (South Sudan), and Bujumbura (Burundi)

Uganda's national carrier, Uganda Airlines, has roared back to business after 18 years of non-existence.

The airline made its maiden flight to Nairobi's Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) on Tuesday, August 27, after leaving Entebbe Airport at around 10am.

The inaugural flight carried selected individuals who included technical officers, government officials and members of the press, Daily Monitor reported.

Ugandan Prime Minister, Ruhakana Rugunda, who presided over the launch of the flight on behalf of President Yoweri Museveni, described the moment as historic and joyous for the people of Uganda and East Africa at large.

Rugunda said the airline will relief Ugandans from high flight charges and travel inconveniences they were being subjected to by foreign companies.

"Each year, Ugandans were donating millions to other countries through foreign travel. Besides, there was a lot of inconvenience to traveling Ugandans by not having direct flights and even being charged discriminate airfares,” Museveni said in his speech read by Rugunda

Uganda airline is also expected to make other trips in Mombasa, Mogadishu (Somalia), Dar Salaam (Tanzania), Juba (South Sudan), Bujumbura (Burundi) and Kilimanjaro in Tanzania.

Uganda Airline collapsed in 2001 and was recently revived by Museveni's government in a bid to bolster the country's economic growth in the region.

"Uganda plays a critical role in the geopolitical and economic stability of East Africa as a region and as a nation, we are strategically placed to serve our neighbors,” said Museveni

In April, the airline received two new CRJ900 Bombardier aircraft and is expecting to receive two more in September.
