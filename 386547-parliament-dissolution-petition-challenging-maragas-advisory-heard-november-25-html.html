Parliament dissolution: Petition challenging Maraga's advisory to be heard from November 25

 Posted on April 25, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  590 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- The dispute will be heard by a five-judge bench comprising of Justice George Odunga, Pauline Nyamweya, Anthony Ndungu, Lydia Achode and James Makau

- The three petitions challenging Chief Justice David Maraga's advisory will be consolidated into one before commencement of the hearing

- The judges will also make a determination on whether or not to enjoin the Law Society of Kenya, Federation of Women lawyers and Kenya Human Rights Commission as interested parties

- Maraga has appointed senior counsel James Oduol to represent him during the hearing of the petitions

The petitions file to challenge Chief Justice David Maraga's advisory to President Uhuru Kenyatta asking him to dissolve parliament for failing to enact the two-thirds gender rule will be heard on Wednesday, November 25.

Five judge-bench

Two Kenyans, Leina Konchella and Abdul Munasar, Attorney General Paul Kihara and MPs through the Parliamentary Service Commission (PSC) moved to court to challenge Maraga's advisory on grounds that it was mischievous and ill-intended.

Justice James Makau who issued the directions on Wednesday when the matters came up for mention said all the issues raised by the petitioners would be deliberate on during November 25 sitting which would be presided over by a five judge-bench appointed by Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu.

The appointed bench comprises of Justice George Odunga, Pauline Nyamweya, Anthony Ndungu, Lydia Achode and James Makau.

Matters for determination

Before the hearing, the five jurists would lead the legal teams in consolidating the three petitions and further make a determination on whether or not to enjoin the Law Society of Kenya, the Federation of Women lawyers and the Kenya Human Rights Commission as interested parties.

The curt will also be required to make a ruling regarding a petition challenging the appointment of the five judges by the deputy Chief Justice.

According to the first petitioner, the appointment of the judges was supposed to be done by the Chief Justice and not his deputy.

Maraga delegated the duty to Mwilu noting he was an interested party in the dispute hence unfit to select a bench that would hear the matter.

The CJ has appointed senior counsel James Oduol to represent him during the hearing of the petitions.

The dispute

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the Chief Justice wrote an advisory opinion to the president asking him to dissolve the 12th parliament for failing to enact the tow-thirds gender bill in the constitution.

Citing Article 261 (7) of the 2010 Constitution, the CJ noted parliament as currently constituted was unconstitutional and should be dissolved.

The opinion was met with resistance by a host of lawmakers who accused Maraga of reading the law selectively and creating a constitutional crisis.

Led by National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturu, the MPs moved to court to challenge the advisory through the PSC.

The lawmaker's petition was filed just days after Konchella and Abdul had submitted theirs. AG Kihara followed suit, bringing the total number of petitions to three within a span of one week.
